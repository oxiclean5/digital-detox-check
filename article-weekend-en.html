Tips for a Smartphone-Free Weekend

Published: July 10, 2025

The weekend is a precious time to recharge. But have you ever found yourself wasting time just looking at your smartphone? This weekend, try stepping away from your phone and enjoy some real rest. Here are 7 easy tips you can try right away.

1. Start your morning with an "analog alarm"

Stopping your phone alarm and immediately checking notifications is the start of the cycle. Try using a simple alarm clock. Just starting your first 10 minutes of the day without your phone can make a big difference.

2. Announce your "no-contact time" in advance

Let your family and friends know in advance—"I won't check my phone on Saturday afternoon!"—so you can enjoy a complete digital detox without anxiety.

3. Use a paper map? Enjoy an analog mini-trip

Take a short walk to a nearby park or café. Try using a paper map from the information center or just walk without a plan. You'll be surprised how much more you notice your surroundings.

4. Immerse yourself in hands-on hobbies

Read a book you haven't picked up in a while, draw, cook, do crafts, or arrange flowers—focus on activities that use your hands. Moving your hands helps you forget the urge to check your phone.

5. Enjoy music without your phone

Instead of a streaming app, try listening to music on an old MP3 player or the radio. You'll discover unexpected songs and be free from the temptation of other apps.

6. Get comfortable with "lounging around"

Maybe you reach for your phone because you don't know how to enjoy just lounging. This weekend, try gazing out the window or lying down without thinking. Give yourself permission to relax—creative ideas will come more easily when your body is at ease.

7. End your day by "writing a journal"

Before bed, open a notebook instead of your phone and jot down your thoughts and feelings from the day. You'll realize how rich a day away from the digital world can be, and it will help you sleep better too.
